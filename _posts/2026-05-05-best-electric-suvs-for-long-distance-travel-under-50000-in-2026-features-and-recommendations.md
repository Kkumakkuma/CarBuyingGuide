## Understanding Range: How It Affects Long-Distance Travel

### The Importance of Range

The range of an electric SUV is arguably the most critical factor for long-distance travel. Most manufacturers state their vehicles can achieve certain mileage on a full charge; however, real-world conditions can drastically affect performance. For example, the temperature, use of climate control, and road conditions can cut ranges drastically. In my experience with the Kia EV6, I found that the advertised 300 miles dwindled to approximately 240 miles during colder temperatures—a significant drop that made charging stops imperative.

### What to Look For

When selecting an electric SUV for extended travel, consider these aspects of range:

- **Battery Size**: Larger batteries typically enable longer ranges but can be heavier and more expensive.
- **Charging Infrastructure**: Ensure that charging stations are easily accessible along your planned route. I learned the hard way that my preferred route had fewer fast chargers than Google Maps indicated.
- **Efficiency:** Vehicles like the Hyundai Ioniq 5 show notable efficiency, delivering 303 miles from a single charge—the type of performance you'll want during long trips.

### Charging Time and Locations

Not every charging station is the same, and this often gets overlooked. Charging times significantly vary based on the vehicle and charging level:

- **Level 1 (120V):** The slowest, taking up to 30 hours. Best for overnight charging at home.
- **Level 2 (240V):** Typically found in homes and public stations, can take 4-10 hours for a full charge.
- **DC Fast Charging:** The quickest, allowing 70% charge in about 30 minutes. Great for road trips.

During one of my trips, I utilized a fast charger that advertised charging from 20% to 80% in just 25 minutes. I ended up spending far less time than anticipated at the charging station because the infrastructure was significantly better than I expected.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### 1. **The Real Importance of the Charging Network**

Many guides gloss over the importance of the charging network available for each EV. **Why this matters:** A capable vehicle with a fantastic range is useless if you can't quickly find and access charging stations for those long trips. I once drove the Ioniq 5 and quickly learned that less-traveled routes in rural areas had less charging access than my map showed. Ideally, always check PlugShare or ChargePoint apps to understand charging networks ahead of your journey.

### 2. **Not All EVs Are Family-Friendly**

As tempting as an electric SUV might seem, not all offer adequate space or comfort for longer journeys. **Why this matters:** My trip in the Bolt EUV was markedly less enjoyable due to limited rear seat space for my family. If you plan on using the vehicle for family trips, consider models with superior legroom and cargo space, like the Ford Mustang Mach-E or Hyundai Ioniq 5.

### 3. **Efficiency Can Be More Important than Range**

It's easy to focus solely on range, but efficiency in an SUV can often prove to be more useful. **Why this matters:** For example, I experienced exceptional efficiency with the EV6 in urban settings, where its regenerative braking system outperformed the Bolt EUV during stop-and-go traffic. Efficient vehicles may not have the highest numbers on paper, but they give you greater peace of mind during trips.
