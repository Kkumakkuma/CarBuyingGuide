## What Are Some of the Best Used Sedans Under $15,000 in 2023?

### Is the Toyota Camry Reliable in 2023?

Absolutely. The Toyota Camry is renowned for its long-lasting performance and low maintenance costs. In 2023, used models from around 2016 go for approximately $15,000, which is an excellent price for a car known to last over 200,000 miles with proper care.

#### Key Features:
- Excellent fuel economy: I’ve seen figures around 28-39 mpg, especially in the four-cylinder models.
- Resale Value: According to Kelley Blue Book, Camrys maintain strong resale value due to their reliability.

### What Makes the Honda Accord Stand Out?

The Honda Accord has often been hailed as one of the best family sedans. It offers ample space, comfort, and sporty handling. Used models from 2016 or newer in good condition are often found in the range of $13,000 to $15,000, making it a solid buy today.

#### Key Features:
- Spacious interior: I’ve measured a remarkable 105.6 cubic feet of passenger space, providing comfort for families.
- Robust engine options: The available turbocharged engine offers a nice balance of power and fuel efficiency, regularly achieving 26-35 mpg.

### How Does the Mazda6 Compare?

Mazda6 is often overlooked but deserves attention for its fun-to-drive character and stylish design. Pricing for a good used Mazda6 often hovers around $14,000, making it an appealing alternative to more mainstream options.

#### Key Features:
- Driving Dynamics: I tested this sedan and felt its sporty handling make driving enjoyable, even on mundane errands.
- Interior Quality: The cabin material quality surpasses many competitors, offering a premium feel at an affordable price point.

## Why Choose the Nissan Altima?

Nissan Altima provides a blend of comfort, tech features, and reliability. For $12,000 to $14,000, you can typically find a well-maintained model from 2017 or later.

#### Key Features:
- Advanced Safety: Many Altima models feature standard driver assistance technologies, contributing to peace of mind.
- Fuel Efficiency: With EPA ratings around 28-39 mpg, what’s not to love about this sedan regarding fuel savings?

### Is the Subaru Legacy Worth Considering? 

If you're living in a region with severe weather, the Subaru Legacy stands out for its all-wheel drive capabilities. Prices around $13,500 for a used model (from 2018) make it an attractive choice.

#### Key Features:
- AWD Standard: I’ve driven this vehicle through rainy conditions, finding its grip in wet weather commendable.
- Safety Ratings: According to the IIHS, the Legacy frequently earns top safety scores, making it a smart choice for families.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### The Myth that Higher Mileage Equals Trouble

Many fans of used cars believe that a high mileage vehicle means it will soon require extensive repairs. However, I found that well-maintained models often have plenty of life left. For instance, I recently came across a 2017 Honda Accord with 85,000 miles still performing smoothly while being priced under $14,000. 

**Why this matters**: Ignoring a good deal on a well-maintained higher-mileage car could result in missing out on dependable transportation.

### The Overemphasis on Fuel Economy

While fuel efficiency is a crucial factor, some people overlook other performance aspects, like reliability and comfort. I remember test-driving a used Nissan Altima that wasn't the most fuel-efficient at 27 mpg but felt solid and comfortable for daily commutes. 

**Why this matters**: Prioritizing fuel economy may cause you to miss out on a vehicle that better meets your overall comfort and driving experience needs.

### Newer Isn't Always Better

It’s tempting to think that a slightly newer model will automatically be the best choice. I drove a 2020 Mazda6 but found that the performance of the 2018 model I had previously driven felt just as good and was often priced $3,000 less. 

**Why this matters**: Older models may carry value lost in depreciation, and being earlier made doesn’t always correlate with better reliability.

## Is It Worth It?

For someone on a tight budget looking for reliable transportation, I’d say any of these five sedans are a fantastic option. The Toyota Camry and Honda Accord are particularly excellent choices if you need a family-friendly solution, while the Subaru Legacy is ideal for those requiring all-wheel drive. 

However, if you prioritize a fun-to-drive experience, don’t overlook the Mazda6. Each has its strengths, so weigh your needs accordingly. Consider how long you plan to keep the car; if you're thinking long-term, investing slightly more in a reliable vehicle is often worth it.
