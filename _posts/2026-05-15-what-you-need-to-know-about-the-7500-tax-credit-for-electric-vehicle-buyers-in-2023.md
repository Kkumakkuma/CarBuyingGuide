## How Does the $7,500 Tax Credit Work?

The $7,500 tax credit operates as a non-refundable tax credit for individuals buying qualified electric vehicles. So, if you buy a new EV that qualifies and your federal tax obligation is less than $7,500, you can only reduce your tax liability to zero; you won’t receive the remaining amount as a refund.

- **Eligibility Requirements:** Not all EVs qualify. Each model must meet specific criteria regarding assembly location and battery capacity; for example, battery capacity must be at least 7 kilowatt-hours (kWh) to count towards the credit.
- **Manufacturer Limitations:** The credit begins to phase out for manufacturers once they sell over 200,000 qualifying vehicles; hence, brands like Tesla and General Motors face restrictions.
- **Claiming the Credit:** You’ll need to fill out IRS Form 8834 when filing your taxes, and exact purchase qualifications exist based on when you bought the EV.

Understanding these rules significantly influenced my decision on which vehicle to purchase.
