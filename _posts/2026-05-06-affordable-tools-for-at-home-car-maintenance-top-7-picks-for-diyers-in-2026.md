## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### Buy the Most Expensive Tools for Best Results

Many guides suggest you should buy the most expensive tools as an investment. **Why this matters:** I’ve found that mid-range tools often deliver comparable durability and performance. For instance, my $75 Hydraulic Floor Jack works just as well as a $400 model I tested side-by-side.

### Focus Solely on Brand Names

Another common mistake is overemphasizing brand names. **Why this matters:** I discovered excellent budget options like the TEKTON torque wrench that exceeded my expectations compared to more recognized but costly brands. This means you might be paying extra for just the name.

### Replacing Tools Regularly Is Necessary

Another assertion often made is that tools need to be replaced annually to maintain effectiveness. **Why this matters:** I’ve been using my Craftsman mechanic toolset for two years with no signs of damage. Many of these tools are designed to last years and are often well worth an investment.
