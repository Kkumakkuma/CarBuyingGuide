## Why Choose a Used SUV for Your Family?

Making the decision to go for a used SUV versus a new one can save families a significant amount. According to Kelley Blue Book, the average new vehicle cost surpassed $48,000 in 2026, in stark contrast to the more budget-friendly options available in the used market. Here are some compelling reasons to consider a used SUV:

### Lower Price, Higher Value

- The depreciation hit of a new vehicle is steep, often losing around 20% of its value within the first year.
- A used SUV often comes loaded with desirable features that are higher trims, which might be unaffordable in a new version.

### Safety Features and Family Comfort

Many used SUVs come equipped with advanced safety features that protect your family. Vehicles manufactured in recent years often have excellent safety technology, including:

- Adaptive cruise control
- Lane-keeping assist
- Automatic emergency braking
- Rearview cameras and Parking sensors

### Versatility

The sheer size of SUVs makes them versatile family companions. They are ideal for various needs such as:

- Carting kids and their friends
- Storage for sports equipment and groceries
- Road trips offering ample passenger space and comfort

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

When I was researching used SUVs for families, I found several misconceptions that merit clarification:

### Myth 1: All SUVs are Gas-Guzzlers

Many guides emphasize the fuel consumption rates of SUVs without providing deeper analyses. 

**Why this matters:** The versatile nature of vehicles like the 2020 Honda CR-V offers around 34 MPG on the highway, making it competitive with many sedans and hybrids. 

### Myth 2: Older Models are Always Less Safe

It's often claimed that only the newest models have adequate safety features.

**Why this matters:** Vehicles such as the 2018 Subaru Forester retain high safety ratings with features like adaptive headlights and advanced airbags. In my experience, older models can provide peace of mind through reliably tested safety features.

### Myth 3: Only New Cars Qualify for Warranty Coverage

Many people believe that used cars risk expensive repairs, but several manufacturers offer certified pre-owned (CPO) programs that include warranties.

**Why this matters:** For example, the Hyundai Santa Fe comes with a 5-year/60,000-mile warranty, even on standard used purchases. This can save you significant repair costs and keep your family safe.

## Is It Worth It?

While buying a used SUV can save you a significant amount of money, it’s essential to determine whether a specific model fits your family's needs. If you’re a family of four who values comfort, safety, and modern amenities without stretching your budget, then a used SUV like the Honda CR-V, Hyundai Santa Fe, or Nissan Rogue could be worth it.

However, if you’re looking for cutting-edge technology and a flashy design that turns heads, you might want to opt for a new vehicle instead. 

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What’s the average price for a used SUV in 2026?

The average price for a used SUV ranges from $20,000 to $30,000 depending on the make, model year, and condition of the vehicle.

### How long do used SUVs last?

Many used SUVs can last well beyond their 100,000-mile mark if they are maintained properly. Brands like Honda and Toyota are known for their longevity and reliability.

### What should I look for when inspecting a used SUV?

Focus on service history, exterior and interior condition, engine lights, and how the vehicle handles during a test drive. A trusted mechanic can also provide critical insights.

### What are typical maintenance costs for used SUVs?

On average, maintenance costs may range from $500 to $800 annually, depending on the make and model. Brands like Honda and Subaru often have lower repairs costs due to their reliability.

### How soon can I expect repairs with a used SUV?

If properly maintained, you might not see significant issues for the first few years of ownership. However, repairs may arise earlier depending on the vehicle's age and condition.
