## The Current Landscape of Car Insurance for Electric Vehicles in 2026

Car insurance for electric vehicles has seen significant shifts in the last couple of years. According to the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC), 2026 saw a nationwide average increase of 12% in insurance premiums for EVs compared to the previous year. Let’s break down some of the key factors impacting these costs.

### Factors Affecting Insurance Premiums

1. **Vehicle Repair Costs**: Electric vehicles typically come with specialized parts and technology, which can lead to higher repair costs after an accident. For instance, a damaged Tesla Model S battery pack can cost between $12,000 and $20,000 to replace, compared to a traditional gasoline engine repair.

2. **Safety Ratings and Driver Behavior**: EVs often have cutting-edge safety features. According to the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS), many electric vehicles like the Ford Mustang Mach-E received commendable safety ratings, which can sometimes result in lower premiums. However, factors such as driving habits still significantly influence your individual rate.

3. **State Regulations and Discounts**: Insurance rates can vary by state due to different regulations and initiatives promoting electric vehicle use. States like California often offer discounts for EV owners, which might reduce the overall insurance premium.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

Not everything you read about EV insurance is accurate. Here are some common misconceptions I encountered during my research, along with why they matter.

### EV Insurance Is Always More Expensive

Contrary to popular belief, EV insurance isn't automatically pricier. Why this matters: Many consumers assume they won’t save on insurance based on previous experiences. For example, I saved nearly $600 when switching from a gasoline car to a Tesla Model 3 policy with Geico.

### All EVs Qualify for the Same Discounts

While some discounts apply to electric vehicles, not all models qualify. Why this matters: Some insurers only provide higher discounts for luxury EVs like Teslas, while economy options might not see similar benefits. This means always checking specifics for the model in question.

### Insurers Factor in Mileage Less for EVs

This is a myth that I found misleading. Why this matters: Many insurers still consider mileage as a risk factor. My Tesla was rated for high mileage, resulting in a higher premium from some providers; check mileage limitations regardless of the vehicle type.

Given that I hadn’t had an accident in years, this program had the potential to reward my driving history financially.

During my first month with DriveEasy, I was pleasantly surprised to find that I had maintained a “Safe Driver” rating, leading to a proactive discount of 15% off my next premium, bringing my Tesla insurance down to $1,487. My experience suggests that UBI is a worthwhile consideration for those who drive less frequently or safely.
