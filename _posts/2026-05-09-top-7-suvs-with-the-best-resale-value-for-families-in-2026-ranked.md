## Which SUVs Retain Value the Best in 2026?

**1. Toyota 4Runner**

### What Makes the 4Runner Stand Out?

The Toyota 4Runner has consistently been a top choice for families valuing durability and reliability. Its robust build and off-road capabilities make it unique in the midsize SUV market.

### Estimated Resale Value

The projected resale value for the 4Runner is about 70% after five years, translating to approximately $38,000 based on an original price of around $54,000. 

### Family-Friendly Features

- **Spacious Interior:** With seating for up to seven people, it's perfect for larger families.
- **Safety:** Toyota Safety Sense suite comes standard, providing advanced safety features like adaptive cruise control and lane-keeping assist.

**2. Honda CR-V**

### Why Is the CR-V So Popular?

Honda’s compact CR-V not only excels in resale value but also combines practicality with comfort. After three years, owner satisfaction ratings remain high, making it a reliable family choice.

### Estimated Resale Value

You can expect the CR-V to retain about 66% of its value, selling for approximately $26,600 after five years, given its initial cost of around $40,000.

### Family-Friendly Features

- **Versatile Cargo Space:** The rear seats fold down conveniently, offering over 75 cubic feet of space for sports gear or camping supplies.
- **Fuel Efficiency:** At around 28 mpg city and 34 mpg highway, it helps families save on fuel costs.

**3. Subaru Forester**

### What Sets the Forester Apart?

Subaru is renowned for its safety features, and the Forester is no exception. Its all-wheel-drive system also makes it exceptionally reliable in various weather conditions, attracting safety-conscious families.

### Estimated Resale Value

The Forester's resale value tends to hover around 63%, with an estimated sale price of around $27,500 after five years.

### Family-Friendly Features

- **EyeSight Safety Technology:** Comes standard and includes features that monitor the road and help prevent accidents.
- **Roomy Interior:** Offers plenty of legroom and headspace, even for taller passengers.

**4. Hyundai Tucson**

### Why Choose the Tucson?

The Hyundai Tucson has been rapidly gaining popularity due to its reasonable price and comprehensive warranty. Its reputation for good resale value has solidified in recent years.

### Estimated Resale Value

The Tucson is expected to retain about 61% of its initial value, approximately $24,500 after five years.

### Family-Friendly Features

- **Smart Cargo Space:** Features an adjustable cargo floor to make loading easier.
- **Technology-Forward Cabin:** The touchscreen interface is intuitive, offering connectivity options that cater to tech-savvy families.

**5. Ford Explorer**

### What Makes the Explorer a Family Favorite?

The Ford Explorer is noted for its powerful engine choices and spacious seating options. It has consistently ranked high for family engagements.

### Estimated Resale Value

With a projected retention of around 60%, Explorers should sell for about $31,200 after five years, assuming an original cost of $52,000.

### Family-Friendly Features

- **Seating Flexibility:** Spacious enough for seven passengers, with various trim levels to suit different needs.
- **Advanced Safety Features:** Standard Ford Co-Pilot360 to enhance safety while traveling.

**6. Toyota RAV4**

### Why Is the Toyota RAV4 So Reliable?

The RAV4 has earned praise for its blend of spaciousness and fuel efficiency. Its resale value is primarily supported by the trust consumers place in the brand.

### Estimated Resale Value

After five years, the RAV4 can expect to sell for about 68% of its original price, which typically hovers around $39,000, equating to roughly $26,520.

### Family-Friendly Features

- **Hybrid Option:** Offers eco-friendly families a hybrid version that offers 40 mpg combined.
- **Safety Ratings:** Consistently receives high marks in safety ratings, making it a solid family choice.

**7. Chevrolet Traverse**

### What Highlights the Traverse?

The Chevrolet Traverse is less common in discussions around resale value but stands out for its passenger capacity and comfort on long trips.

### Estimated Resale Value

### Family-Friendly Features

- **Generous Cargo Capacity:** Offers more cargo space than many competitors in its segment.
- **Third-Row Space:** Suitable for adults, making long trips comfortable for larger families.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### SUVs Under $30,000 Truly Hold Value

Many guides suggest that high-reputation brands automatically retain value, ignoring affordable alternatives. **Why this matters:** I’ve seen reliable SUVs like the Toyota RAV4 offer solid depreciation even at higher initial costs. By considering options priced under $30,000, families don't have to sacrifice quality for affordability.

### Newer Doesn’t Always Mean Better Resale

It's a common myth that the newest models will always have the best resale value. **Why this matters:** I learned this firsthand when the 2021 model of a less popular brand was deeply discounted while earlier models retained higher percentage values. Often, reputable older models can be a great investment.

### Condition Over Brand Reputation

Many guides focus heavily on brand while neglecting the particular condition of individual vehicles. **Why this matters:** During my recent research, I discovered that a well-maintained 2017 Subaru Forester could outvalue a poorly kept 2019 model of a highly appraised brand. Condition makes a critical difference.

## Is It Worth It?

If your family prioritizes safety, cargo space, and convenience in vehicle purchases, investing in one of the vehicles listed above is worth the money. In my opinion, the Toyota RAV4 is particularly strong for families who appreciate both fuel efficiency and resale value. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### How Much Do These SUVs Cost in 2026?

The prices for the featured SUVs range from approximately $39,000 for the Toyota RAV4 to $55,000 for the Chevrolet Traverse, depending on the model and added features.

### When Should I Sell My SUV to Get the Best Resale Value?

The optimal selling window is generally between three to five years after purchasing. That’s when depreciation plateaus, and the vehicle still boasts high demand due to its features.

### Are Hybrid SUVs Better for Resale Value?

Yes, hybrid SUVs, such as the Honda CR-V Hybrid, tend to retain higher resale values due to increasing consumer interest in fuel efficiency.

### How Long Will It Take to See the Resale Benefits?

You can typically start seeing significant resale benefits after about three years of ownership, especially for brands like Subaru and Toyota known for lower depreciation.

### What Factors Influence Resale Value?

Factors including mileage, condition, brand reputation, and market trends significantly affect resale value. As I noted in my research, a well-maintained car can command higher prices consistently.

### Considerations for Selling Your SUV

As I prepared for the sale, I learned a few important lessons about maximizing resale value:

- **Timing Matters:** The time of year can have a significant effect on resale values, especially for SUVs which tend to be more sought after before summer road trips.
  
- **Maintenance Records:** Keeping up with regular maintenance and having records ready can reassure buyers about the vehicle's reliability, which is critical for family-oriented buyers. 

- **Transparency is Key:** When chatting with potential buyers, being open about any previous issues or repairs builds trust, and often results in a smoother transaction.
